Chapter 10.5, Problem 22E
Problem

A connected graph has twelve vertices and eleven edges. Does it have a vertex of degree 1? Why?

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 2

    A graph G is said to be connected if between any two vertices in the graph, it is possible to traverse from one vertex to another though a sequence of adjacent edges present in the graph.

  2. Step 2 of 2

    Refer to the Theorem 11.5.4.

    Since, the given graph G is connected and has 12 vertices and 11 edges, so by the Theorem 11.5.4 the graph G is a tree.

    Now refer to the Lemma 11.5.1.

    Since the graph G is a tree and it contains more than one vertex, so by Lemma 11.5.1 the graph has at least one vertex of degree 1.
