Chapter 5.9, Problem 4E
Problem

The set of arithmetic expressions over the real numbers can be defined recursively as follows:

I. BASE: Each real number r is an arithmetic expression.

II. RECURSION: If u and v are arithmetic expressions, then the following are also arithmetic expressions:

a. (+u)

b. (−u)

c. (u + v)

d. (u − v)

e. (u ·v)

III. RESTRICTION: There are no arithmetic expressions over the real numbers other than those obtained from I and II.

(Note that the expression is legal even though the value of v may be 0.) Give derivations showing that each of the following is an arithmetic expression.
