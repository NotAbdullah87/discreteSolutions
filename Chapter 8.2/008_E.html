Chapter 8.2, Problem 8E
Problem

A number of relations are defined on the set A = {0, 1, 2, 3}. For each relation:

a. Draw the directed graph.

b. Determine whether the relation is reflexive.

c. Determine whether the relation is symmetric.

d. Determine whether the relation is transitive.

Give a counterexample in each case in which the relation does not satisfy one of the properties.

Exercise

R8 = {(0, 0),(1, 1)}

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 6

    The definitions of reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are universal statements. This implies that to prove a relation has one of the properties, use either the method of exhaustion or the method of generalizing from the generic particular.
