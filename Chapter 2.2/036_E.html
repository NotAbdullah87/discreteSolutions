Chapter 2.2, Problem 36E
Problem

Taking the long view on your education, you go to the Prestige Corporation and ask what you should do in college to be hired when you graduate. The personnel director replies that you will be hired only if you major in mathematics or computer science, get a B average or better, and take accounting. You do, in fact, become a math major, get a B+ average, and take accounting. You return to Prestige Corporation, make a formal application, and are turned down. Did the personnel director lie to you?

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 3

    List the given data,

    The personnel director’s reply: “you will be hired only if you major in mathematics or computer science, get a B average or better, and take accounting”.

    Suppose you become a math major, got a B+ average, and, taken accounting.

    And, you return to the Prestige Corporation, make a formal application, and, are turned down.

    Need to determine whether the personnel director lies or not.

  2. Step 2 of 3

    In general, “” can be interpreted as if P, then Q.

    And, “P Only if Q “is equivalent to, and, (if not Q, then, not P).

    Assume,

    P: “You are hired”

    Q: “you major in mathematics or computer science, get a B average or better, and take accounting”.

    Then, is,

    “If you didn't fulfill any of the requirements, then, you would not be hired.”

    However, nothing is said what should happen if you have fulfilled the requirements.

  3. Step 3 of 3

    The Director would have lied to you in two cases:

    1. If is false,

    2. And is true.

    In general,

    The expression means, you are hired without fulfilling all the requirements. Since this is not the case, the Director has not lied to you.

    So, in view of this, the turning down of the formal application is not a lie.

    Hence, the director didn’t lie to you.
