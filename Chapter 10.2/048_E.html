    Def: 1: Let G be a graph. An Euler circuit for G is a circuit that contains every vertex and every edge of G. that is an Euler circuit for G is a sequence of adjacent vertices and edges in G that has at least one edge, starts and ends at the same vertex, uses every vertex of G at least once, and uses every edge of G exactly once.

    Def: 2: Given a graph G, a Hamiltonian circuit for G is a simple circuit that includes every vertex of G. that is a Hamiltonian circuit for G is a sequence of adjacent vertices and distinct edges in which every vertex of G appears exactly once, except for the first and the last.

    Theorem 1: a connected graph has an Euler circuit if and only if every vertex of the graph has positive even degree.

    Theorem 2: if a graph G has a Hamiltonian circuit, then G has a sub graph H with the properties

    (i) H contains every vertex of G

    (ii) H is connected

    (iii) H has the same number of vertices and edges

    (iv) Every vertex of H is of degree 2
