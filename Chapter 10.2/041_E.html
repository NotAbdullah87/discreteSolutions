Chapter 10.2, Problem 41E
Problem

Prove that if there is a trail in a graph G from a vertex v to a vertex w, then there is a trail from w to v.

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    Def: let G be a graph and let v and w be vertices in G, a trail from v to w is a path that does not contain a repeated edge.

    (Note that there can be a repeated vertex on the path)

    In view of this definition, we prove the given statement.

    Suppose v and w are any vertices of the graph G which have a trail from v to w

    i.e., there is a walk of the form that connects v, w and there can be some of the vertices repeated on the path and no repeated edge.

    By reversing the path, we can see, it becomes a path between w and v without repeated edges.

    So, this can be interpreted as the trail from w to v.

    Thus, whenever there is a trail from v to w, there is a trail from w to v.

    Hence the theorem
