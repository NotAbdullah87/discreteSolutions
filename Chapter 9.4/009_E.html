Chapter 9.4, Problem 9E
Problem

a. If seven integers are chosen from between 1 and 12 inclusive, must at least one of them be odd? Why?

b. If ten integers are chosen from between 1 and 20 inclusive, must at least one of them be even? Why?

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 3

    Pigeon holes principal: If objects are put in to containers with then at least one container must contain more than one object.

  2. Step 2 of 3

    (a)

    Consider the problem that if seven integers are chosen from between 1 and 12 inclusive then objective is show that at least one of them must be odd.

    For this note that there are six odd and six even numbers from 1 to 12 therefore if, seven integers are chosen from between 1 and 12 then the pigeonhole principal guarantees that there must be at least one odd integer.
