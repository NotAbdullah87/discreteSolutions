Chapter 9.4, Problem 27E
Problem

In a group of 2,000 people, must at least 5 have the same birthday? Why?

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    A leap year consists of 366 different days. By Generalized Pigeonhole principle, in a group of people, there would be at least people have the same birthday.

    Therefore, when four people were considered to be born on the same day then it implies that there are people.

    From this at least five people have the same birthday.

    Suppose there are more than 1465 people, at least 5 people have the same birthday.

    Indeed, with 2000 people at least 6 people have the same birthday.
