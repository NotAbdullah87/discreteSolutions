Problem

Exercise refers to the Euler phi function, denoted ϕ, which is defined as follows: For each integer n ≥ 1, ϕ(n) is the number of positive integers less than or equal to n that have no common factors with n except ±1. For example, ϕ(10) = 4 because there are four positive integers less than or equal to 10 that have no common factors with 10 except ±1; namely, 1, 3, 7, and 9.
