The Euclidean Algorithm

The greatest common divisor of two integers a and b is the largest integer that divides both a and b. For example, the greatest common divisor of 12 and 30 is 6. The Euclidean algorithm provides a very efficient way to compute the greatest common divisor of two integers.

Definition

Let a and b be integers that are not both zero. The greatest common divisor of aand b, denoted gcd(a, b), is the integer d with the following properties:

1. d is a common divisor of both a and b. In other words,

d | a and d | b.

2. For all integers c, if c is a common divisor of both a and b, then c is less than or equal to d. In other words,

Integers a and b are called relatively prime if, and only if, their greatest common divisor is 1.
