Problem

Rewrite the statement “No good cars are cheap” in the form “∀x, if P(x) then ~Q(x).” Indicate whether each of the following arguments is valid or invalid, and justify your answers.

a. No good car is cheap.

A Rimbaud is a good car.

∴ A Rimbaud is not cheap.

b. No good car is cheap.

A Simbaru is not cheap.

∴ A Simbaru is a good car.

c. No good car is cheap.

A VX Roadster is cheap.

∴ A VX Roadster is not good.

d. No good car is cheap.

An Omnex is not a good car.

∴ An Omnex is cheap.

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 5

    To say that the argument is valid concludes that its form is also valid.

    If the resulted premise statements are all true, then the conclusion is also true.
