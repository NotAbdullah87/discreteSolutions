Problem

Imagine that the diagram shown below is a map with countries labeled a-g. Is it possible to color the map with only three colors so that no two adjacent countries have the same color? To answer this question, draw and analyze a graph in which each country is represented by a vertex and two vertices are connected by an edge if, and only if, the countries share a common border.

  1. Step 1 of 4

  2. Step 2 of 4

    Let we have three colors: color 1, color 2, color 3.

    The maximum degree vertex in the graph is e hence color this vertex with color one. The vertex a is not connected to vertex e with an edge and hence color 2 may also be used to color the vertex a.

    From the remaining uncolored vertices d, g and f. These are having the maximal degree 4, 3, 3 correspondingly. Choose any one vertex of them. We have chosen d and use color 2 to color it.

  3. Step 3 of 4

    From the graph, it is clear that the vertices b, c and f share an edge with each other. This concludes that color 2 may be used for only one of vertex c or vertex f.

    Now we color the vertex b with color 2. Now color 2 is used to color the vertex f because the degree of vertex f is greater than the degree of vertex c.

  4. Step 4 of 4

    In the remaining uncolored vertices of the Graph G, vertex g has a maximal degree so color we color this vertex with color 3. Then, we observe that the vertex g does not share an edge with vertex c, color 3 may can be used to color the vertex c. Now, all the vertices of the graph G are colored.

    Therefore, it is possible to color the map with only three colors.
